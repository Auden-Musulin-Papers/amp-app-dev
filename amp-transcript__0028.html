0006 Mendelson edited Auden´s early works and wrote superb commentaries.

0008 theses have been out here and have shaken us up, particularly
0009 Michael O´Sullivan of Trinity College, Dublin who - not speaking
0010 a word of German - organised a full-scale exhibition and an
0011 international symposium in Vienna.  The editor of this volume
0012 Peter Müller had brought Michael out to see me at Friedau in the
0013 preceding year;  now he came again, and after the closure,
0014 exhausted, he spent a long weekend in the country to recover.
0015 We dug out ancient files containing original Auden manuscripts,
0016 letters, personal notes and newspaper cuttings, and now we tried
0017 to winnow the wheat from the chaff.  After some general discussion
0018 it seemed to us all that there remain certain aspects of Auden´s
0019 life in Lower Austria which are not on record, or where they are,
0020 not from the worm´s eye view.  As an older man he was happier
0021 here than anywhere else:  he felt at home.  He was at Kirchstetten
0022 not only during the summer, ias is often said, but with
0023 interruptions for five months, depending on his engagements.
0024 In the course of these years he was still highly productive
0025 creative.  So that it might, we thought, be of value to put some
0026 of those things on record which would otherwise be lost.  Not
0027 for the first time self.-criticism was expressed, but while doubts
0028 concerning self-importancesand sell-out of firiendship were not
0029 entirely banished, the view prevailed that the local witness
0030 needs to be put on paper.  Scholarship is at work elsewhere;
0031 here a few appendices and footnotes are on offer.
0032      Auden´s opinions on biographies of creative artists in general
0033 were, as Humphrey Carpenter pointed out, highly contradictory.
0034 Again and again he said that the private life of poets and other
0035 people engaged in creative work is none of the public´s business
0036 but he also said:´`The biography of an artist, if his life as a whole
0037 was sufficiently interesting, is permissible, provided that the
0038 biographer and his readers realise that such an account throws no
0039 light whatsoever upon the artist´s work."  And "I do believe,
0040 however, that, more often than most people realise, khis works
0041 may throw light upon his life."
